Boron steel

More steel means more protection. But the conundrum for our engineers was, how do we give you extra protection without adding extra weight that could cost you fuel savings? They looked to the aerospace industry for the answer and found it in boron steel.

DIG DEEPER

4 x stronger than normal high-strength steel

  • Boron steel, used in the aerospace industry, is one of the ultra-high-strength steels Ford uses, along with low-alloy and dual-phase steels, to protect you without adding weight.
  • These steels are used to reinforce key structural areas to provide a 'safety cell'.
  • Their high yield and tensile strengths allow us to form complex shapes that can be welded into key areas, such as pillars and bumpers.
  • Boron steel can be melded into thinner components than normal steel, so theyâ€™re lighter but still retain their strength and stiffness.
  • Boron steel is used in the door intrusion beams of all Ford vehicles to help protect passengers in the event of a side impact, as well as other keys areas on specific models, depending on their size and usage.

Did you know?

Boron steel is used in widely diverse ways, from enamels and golf clubs, to pyrotechnics and nuclear reactors.
