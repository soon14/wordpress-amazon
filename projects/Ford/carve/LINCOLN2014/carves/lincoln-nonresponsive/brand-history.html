This Week in History

Founded in 1917, the Lincoln Motor Company has been the standard of elegance and definition of what a luxury car could be. Lincoln is made up of these great stories that shaped it in the past century.

A Design for the Ages

April 29, 1937.

Eugene “Bob” Gregorie, one of Lincoln’s head designers, submits a patent for the design of his radical Lincoln Zephyr. Response to the Zephyr was swift, revolutionizing long-held notions of automotive design at the time. With its graceful, sweeping lines reminiscent of an inverted ship’s prow, the Zephyr captivated the public for years.

The Museum of Modern Art later called the Zephyr the first-ever streamlined car to achieve success in the United States.
